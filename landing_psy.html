Psychology Research

Data Science Methods for Psychology

Social Behavior and Psychological Dynamics Analysis

Project Overview:

This project is conducted at a leading global institution for psychological research, focusing on using computational techniques to analyze and predict social psychological behaviors. Integrating social psychology, cognitive science, and artificial intelligence, the project aims to deepen understanding of the psychological mechanisms at play in individual and group social interactions and explore how these mechanisms influence human behavior and societal structures.

Research Methods:

  • Social Behavior Data Analysis: Collecting and analyzing behavior data from social interactions, group activities, and public settings to identify social psychological phenomena.
  • Group Dynamics Identification: Using computational models to study emotional contagion and behavioral resonance within groups, exploring psychological responses under social influences.
  • Social Attitudes and Bias Prediction: Developing predictive models to analyze the formation of social attitudes and biases and their impact on individual and group behaviors.
  • Social Psychological Intervention Assessment: Evaluating the effectiveness of psychological interventions in altering social behaviors and promoting group harmony.

Significance:

By revealing the psychological dynamics in social interactions, this project not only deepens theoretical research in social psychology but also provides practical applications for addressing social issues such as prejudice, discrimination, and group conflicts. The findings will aid in developing new intervention strategies to promote social harmony and individual psychological health.

Student Requirements:

Students participating in this project need to have or be willing to develop quantitative and computational skills, including data processing, statistical analysis, and machine learning techniques. The project encourages those who are not yet proficient in these skills but have a strong desire to learn to join and will provide necessary training and guidance. This offers students a valuable opportunity to enhance their skills through hands-on operations and research activities, while promoting critical thinking and problem-solving abilities.

STARC Application Process

Submit a Research Profile

Share your academic background, areas of interest, and any prior experience with research or independent projects. No need for polished credentials—just show us how you think.

Statement of Purpose

Tell us what excites you about research and why you're ready to take on a real project. We look for clarity, curiosity, and commitment—not perfection.

Project Matching & Review

Our team reviews your materials and matches you with a research direction and mentor aligned with your strengths and goals. Select applicants may also be invited for a short conversation with our advisors.

Mentor Invitation

Once matched, you'll receive a formal invitation to join a live project led by a university researcher. This is not a training simulation—you’ll be contributing to real work.

Begin Your STARC Journey

You’ll join a structured research team, receive mentorship, collaborate on defined tasks, and grow as an independent thinker and contributor.
